Logo Description & Rationale

The logo is designed to be reminiscent of a simplified form of a duck, specifically a mallard. It encompasses our overarching theme of our app being visually centered around ducks while also being simplistic design-wise with shapes to convey feelings of comfort through easy to read design.

Wordmark Rationale

When choosing the font intended for the wordmark, Fredoka (or otherwise known as Fredoka One), one of the main reasons for choosing the font was it's visual "friendliness". Most of our fonts were chosen based on two factors that represented that "friendliness": roundness and sans serif style.

Colour choice wise, the decision to use predominantly use our pre-existing palette was set in stone to convey visual coherence within our app's theme.

Colour Palette

FLUSH ORANGE

#FF7A00

The flush vibrant orange largely is in reference to duck beaks which aligns with our overall thematic of our app surrounding ducks. This highly accented color can help identify key features of our app by being very visible

SEASHELL

#FEFBF9

The utilization of this colour helps alleviate the strain that pure white causes. Seashell will be our primary colour and will compose the majority of the screen. Additionally, its colour also matches the tone of Flush Orange, which together composes the basic palette of a real duck.

GRAY5

#0E0D0D

This colour will compose the majority of our text, being our primary body color. The colour comes from the eyes of a duck which are predominantly black.

BUTTON BLUE

#258FF1

This colour will be our secondary accent, it’s origin comes from the vast light blue of a pond in which ducks traverse through. It will be included in our patterns and designs

ARATHI HIGHLANDS

#8DA742

Arathi Highlands will be utilized as an additional text colour to place emphasis on text. The color originates from the idea of “Without land, there is no pond”. It’ll be acting as a compliment to Button Blue being able to mingle with it as a pattern.

Icons

All icons used will be common visuals elements that associate themselves with their tasks. Affordances towards these icons should be predictable, or easily learnable.

Profile

Representing our profile page, it is generic while also being recognizeable as a silhouette of a peson. It promotes inclusivity through unspecificity and accessibility through it's generic visual

Settings

Representing our setting's page, it uses the commonly associate cogwheel to lead users to understand that this icon represent's technical internal change.

Close/Exit

Representing the action of exiting a page, this visual commonly is associated with stopping an action

Pause

Representing the action of stopping a process, animation or movement. This icon is the most commonly associated visual related to that action, being most prominent on all major video platforms. By using this we are generating predictiblity within our use of icons

Play

Representing the action of starting a process, animation, or movement. Like the pause icon, it is the most commonly associated visual with this action. We are generating accessibility through the same way as well
